# Dungeon Drifters Progression, Difficulty, and Expedition Economy

## Core Principle

Dungeon Drifters should always allow the player to enter and play dungeons.

Dungeon runs do not consume energy.

Expeditions consume a regenerating energy resource.

As the player advances, dungeons become increasingly punishing. Continued progress requires stronger characters, improved equipment, better party composition, more resources, and deeper knowledge of dungeon mechanics.

The player can overcome this pressure through:

* active dungeon grinding
* expeditions
* strategic party rotation
* equipment upgrades
* crafting
* improved character builds
* better dungeon preparation
* optional paid acceleration

The game does not stop the player from playing.

It pressures the player to decide how they want to progress.

---

# 1. The Two Progression Paths

The player always has two primary ways to improve the roster.

## Active Dungeon Grinding

The player directly enters dungeons and earns progress through gameplay.

Dungeon grinding provides:

* full experience for the active pair
* common materials
* currency
* equipment drops
* consumables
* combat practice
* dungeon knowledge
* relationship development
* occasional rare rewards

Dungeon grinding costs time and attention but does not consume energy.

A player who does not want to spend money can continue progressing by playing more dungeon runs.

## Expeditions

The inactive pair is sent on an off-screen assignment.

Expeditions provide:

* reduced character experience
* targeted materials
* equipment
* crafting resources
* scouting information
* maps
* rare discoveries
* consumables
* special encounters
* expedition-specific rewards

Expeditions consume energy.

The player may wait for energy to regenerate, spend earned resources to restore some energy, or purchase optional acceleration.

---

# 2. Expedition Energy

Expedition energy is tied only to the expedition system.

It does not prevent the player from:

* entering dungeons
* fighting enemies
* advancing character relationships
* testing party combinations
* collecting normal dungeon rewards
* grinding experience manually
* attempting difficult content

When expedition energy is empty, the player can continue playing Dungeon Drifters.

The limitation is that the inactive pair cannot begin another full expedition until energy is available.

This creates pressure without creating a complete play barrier.

---

# 3. Why Expeditions Matter

Expeditions are not optional background flavor.

They become increasingly important as the player moves into harder regions.

Early dungeons can be cleared with basic leveling and ordinary equipment.

Later dungeons require more specialized preparation.

The player may need:

* resistance equipment
* upgraded weapons
* rare crafting materials
* stronger consumables
* character-specific gear
* knowledge of enemy weaknesses
* hidden routes
* boss preparation items
* improved expedition rewards
* a more balanced roster

Expeditions provide faster or more targeted access to these resources than ordinary dungeon grinding.

The player can still grind manually, but expeditions reduce the amount of repeated dungeon play required.

---

# 4. Progressive Dungeon Pressure

Each new region should increase difficulty through more than higher enemy statistics.

## Increased Enemy Strength

Enemies gain:

* more health
* stronger attacks
* improved defenses
* more complex abilities
* better coordination
* resistance to simple strategies

## Environmental Punishment

Later dungeons introduce conditions such as:

* poison
* extreme heat or cold
* flooding
* darkness
* unstable terrain
* magical corruption
* limited healing
* mana disruption
* equipment degradation
* persistent injuries

## Resource Attrition

The player must manage:

* health
* mana
* consumables
* durability
* status effects
* limited rest opportunities
* retreat decisions

## Character Matchups

Certain enemies and environments heavily favor particular Drifters or party combinations.

Using the wrong pair remains possible, but requires:

* better equipment
* higher levels
* stronger consumables
* greater player skill
* additional grinding

## Roster Requirements

Story dungeons may require specific characters.

Full-party bosses require all four Drifters to remain viable.

Players who rely exclusively on one preferred pair eventually face pressure to train and equip the rest of the roster.

---

# 5. Soft Progression Walls

Dungeon Drifters should primarily use soft walls rather than hard power gates.

A hard wall prevents entry.

Example:

> Party level too low. Entry denied.

A soft wall allows entry but makes the consequences clear.

Example:

> The enemies resist your primary damage types, the environment drains mana, and your equipment lacks corruption resistance.

The player may still attempt the dungeon.

A skilled, prepared, or stubborn player might succeed early.

Most players will respond by:

* grinding levels
* changing party members
* upgrading equipment
* crafting resistance items
* completing expeditions
* gathering information
* changing tactics

Soft walls preserve player agency while still supporting progression pressure.

---

# 6. Difficulty Curve

## Early Game

The early game teaches the full system.

Characteristics:

* most pairings are viable
* enemies have simple weaknesses
* expeditions are inexpensive
* energy feels generous
* gear upgrades are straightforward
* level differences remain small
* mistakes are recoverable
* dungeon grinding produces meaningful progress quickly

The player learns:

* how each Drifter works
* how active and expedition parties interact
* how equipment affects combat
* how dungeon traits influence party choice
* how expeditions support progression

## Early Midgame

The game begins asking the player to make deliberate choices.

Characteristics:

* dungeon weaknesses matter more
* some characters begin falling behind
* basic equipment becomes insufficient
* expeditions provide useful targeted materials
* party synergy becomes important
* careless resource use causes failed runs

The player begins rotating characters and planning deployments rather than simply selecting favorites.

## Late Midgame

The progression economy becomes central.

Characteristics:

* dungeons require specialized gear
* rare materials become necessary
* enemy mechanics punish poor matchups
* expedition selection affects future dungeon success
* underdeveloped Drifters create problems in required story content
* boss preparation may require several dungeon and expedition cycles

The player can still grind normally, but targeted expeditions save substantial time.

## Late Game

Late-game progression demands full system mastery.

Characteristics:

* strong party specialization
* dangerous environmental effects
* layered enemy mechanics
* demanding equipment requirements
* limited room for poorly developed characters
* full-party bosses that test the complete roster
* high-value expeditions
* rare crafting resources
* difficult optional dungeons

The player must understand both combat and roster management.

## Endgame

Endgame content supports long-term play.

Possible systems include:

* rotating challenge dungeons
* elite expeditions
* rare gear sets
* advanced crafting
* high-difficulty bosses
* seasonal modifiers
* prestige progression
* score-based dungeon runs
* restricted party challenges
* endless dungeon modes
* account-wide cosmetic rewards

---

# 7. The Progression Decision

When the player reaches a difficult dungeon, they have several options.

## Push Forward

Attempt the dungeon immediately.

Advantages:

* possible early victory
* no delay
* greater challenge
* potential access to higher-tier rewards

Risks:

* failed run
* wasted consumables
* injuries
* lost resources
* slower overall progression

## Grind Dungeons

Repeat available content manually.

Advantages:

* unlimited access
* full active-party experience
* direct control over progression
* no expedition energy required
* additional practice

Costs:

* greater time investment
* repeated content
* slower access to targeted rare materials

## Run Expeditions

Send the inactive pair after specific resources.

Advantages:

* targeted rewards
* passive roster experience
* gear and material acquisition
* scouting advantages
* reduced manual grinding

Costs:

* expedition energy
* delayed resolution
* lower experience than active dungeon play

## Change Party Composition

Bring characters better suited to the dungeon.

Advantages:

* reduced need for raw statistical power
* access to better counters
* new pairing synergies
* more efficient progression

Costs:

* weaker or less familiar characters may require training
* equipment must be redistributed
* the preferred pair may be temporarily separated

## Improve Preparation

Craft items, change equipment, study enemy information, or unlock new abilities.

Advantages:

* rewards planning
* reduces reliance on raw level grinding
* supports different playstyles

Costs:

* consumes resources
* may require previous expeditions or dungeon runs

---

# 8. Expedition Energy Economy

The energy economy should be generous enough that expeditions feel like part of normal play.

It should not feel as though the game immediately demands payment.

## Energy Regeneration

Energy regenerates over time.

Possible sources of additional energy include:

* daily login rewards
* story progression
* dungeon milestones
* boss victories
* achievements
* level rewards
* rare dungeon drops
* limited free refills
* optional purchases

## Expedition Costs

Expedition costs may vary based on:

* expedition length
* danger level
* reward quality
* destination
* selected characters
* special modifiers
* guaranteed rewards

A common material expedition may cost little energy.

A rare artifact expedition may consume a significant portion of the player’s energy capacity.

## Energy Capacity

Energy capacity may increase through:

* account progression
* hub upgrades
* story milestones
* achievements
* optional permanent purchases

Capacity increases should provide convenience without making the base system unusable.

---

# 9. Expedition Categories

Expeditions should allow the player to target current progression needs.

## Training Expedition

Primary reward:

* experience

Used to help inactive or underleveled Drifters remain viable.

## Material Expedition

Primary reward:

* crafting components

Used for weapons, armor, consumables, and resistance gear.

## Equipment Expedition

Primary reward:

* weapons, armor, relics, or upgrade components

Higher-level versions may require more energy and carry more risk.

## Scouting Expedition

Primary reward:

* dungeon information

May reveal:

* enemy types
* weaknesses
* hazards
* hidden paths
* boss mechanics
* treasure locations

## Recovery Expedition

Primary reward:

* consumables and supplies

Useful before demanding dungeon runs or boss encounters.

## Story Expedition

Primary reward:

* narrative progress
* character events
* hidden lore
* new locations

These may be limited and should not require payment to complete.

## Elite Expedition

Primary reward:

* rare or endgame resources

These consume more energy and may require specific character combinations or roster strength.

---

# 10. Monetization Loop

The monetization loop is built around expedition acceleration, not access to dungeon gameplay.

## Free Player Loop

A free player can:

* play unlimited dungeons
* earn full experience manually
* collect normal materials
* wait for expedition energy
* use earned energy refills
* rotate characters
* grind difficult content
* complete the story
* defeat bosses
* develop the entire roster

Progress may be slower, but it remains fully possible.

## Paying Player Loop

A paying player may choose to:

* refill expedition energy
* increase energy capacity
* complete more expeditions
* gain targeted materials faster
* reduce required grinding
* accelerate roster development
* purchase cosmetic content
* purchase optional convenience features

Payment improves efficiency and reduces repetition.

It does not replace playing the game.

---

# 11. Monetization Boundaries

The system should avoid making payment feel mandatory for basic play.

The player should not be required to pay to:

* enter normal dungeons
* continue the main story
* use a specific Drifter
* participate in boss battles
* unlock essential mechanics
* recover from ordinary failure
* obtain basic progression materials
* keep the game playable

The strongest monetization targets are:

* expedition energy refills
* increased energy capacity
* additional expedition options
* expedition reward bonuses
* cosmetics
* camp decorations
* character skins
* visual effects
* optional passes
* convenience bundles

Direct combat power should be handled carefully.

A paid advantage that completely bypasses dungeon preparation would undermine the central gameplay loop.

---

# 12. Failure and Recovery

Failed dungeon runs should create pressure without destroying progress.

Possible consequences include:

* consumed items remain spent
* damaged equipment
* temporary injuries
* partial loss of collected rewards
* forced retreat
* expedition opportunity cost
* delayed boss progress

The player should still retain something from most failed runs:

* experience
* discovered information
* map progress
* enemy knowledge
* partial materials
* relationship progress

Failure should encourage adaptation rather than abandonment.

After failure, the player may:

* grind
* run an expedition
* change characters
* craft equipment
* gather information
* improve abilities
* try another dungeon

---

# 13. Example Progression Wall

The player reaches the Ashen Vault.

Known conditions:

* enemies resist physical damage
* heat causes persistent health loss
* healing items are less effective
* the dungeon contains magical armor

The player’s preferred team is Branoc and Zhaivra.

They attempt the dungeon and fail.

The player now has several options.

## Option One: Grind

Run previous dungeons with Branoc and Zhaivra to gain levels and collect more consumables.

## Option Two: Rotate

Bring Azhvielle for stronger magical damage and pair her with Branoc for protection.

## Option Three: Expeditions

Send Zhaivra and Joruun on a material expedition for heat-resistant crafting components.

## Option Four: Scout

Complete a scouting expedition to reveal a hidden route that avoids the worst environmental damage.

## Option Five: Upgrade

Use existing materials to craft heat-resistant armor and magical damage consumables.

A free player can solve the wall through additional play.

A paying player may accelerate the expedition portion.

The dungeon remains playable in either case.

---

# 14. Boss Preparation Loop

Bosses should represent the highest point of progression pressure within a region.

Before a boss, the player may need to:

* train all four Drifters
* complete character-specific dungeons
* gather boss weakness information
* craft resistance equipment
* recover consumables
* upgrade abilities
* complete special expeditions
* locate the boss arena
* weaken environmental hazards
* distribute equipment across the full team

All four characters then enter the boss battle.

The boss tests whether the player prepared the entire roster rather than only a preferred pair.

---

# 15. Complete Mobile Progression Loop

The complete loop becomes:

**Choose a dungeon
→ review strengths, weaknesses, and story restrictions
→ select an active pair
→ assign the other pair to an expedition
→ spend expedition energy
→ complete the dungeon run
→ earn active rewards
→ resolve expedition rewards
→ return to the hub
→ level characters
→ craft and upgrade equipment
→ redistribute resources
→ confront increasing difficulty
→ grind, rotate, or run more expeditions
→ prepare all four Drifters
→ enter the full-party boss battle
→ unlock the next region
→ repeat at a higher level of pressure**

---

# 16. Core Economic Identity

Dungeon Drifters does not monetize the player’s ability to play.

It monetizes acceleration within the roster-management economy.

The player can always enter dungeons.

The player can always grind.

The player can always improve through active play.

Expeditions provide a faster, targeted, and more convenient progression route.

As dungeons become increasingly punishing, expeditions become increasingly valuable.

The central economic tension is:

> Do I spend more time grinding the resources I need, or do I use expedition energy to acquire them more efficiently?

That decision supports both the gameplay loop and the mobile economy.
